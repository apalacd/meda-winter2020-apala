Helen Keller

A deaf-blind revolutionary and social activist

Here's a time line of Helen Keller's life:

  • 1880 - Born on 27 June in Tuscumbia, Alabama.
  • 1881 - Experiences a severe childhood illness, which left her deaf and blind.
  • 1886 - Meets Alexander Graham Bell, who investigates issues of deafness and sound (he would also develop the first telephone).
  • 1888 - Visits Perkins Institute for the Blind and meets with 20 years old former student Anne Sullivan, also visually impaired and with no prior experience, teaches Helen how to communicate.
  • 1891 - Becomes proficient in Brail and publishes a book "The Frost King" at age 11.
  • 1894 - Attends Wright-Humason School for the Deaf and Horace Mann School for the Deaf and learns to speak and practise lip reading.
  • 1896 - Attends The Cambridge School for Young Ladies and becomes proficient in writing with a Braille typewriter.
  • 1900 - Gets admission in Radcliffe College of Harvard University where the admirer American author, Mark Twain helps persuade Henry Rogers, an oil businessman to fund education.
  • 1903 - Becomes proficient writer and speaker at age 22 and publishes an autobiography “The Story of My Life” and essays on “Optimism”.
  • 1904 - Graduates with a Bachelor of Arts degree from Radcliffe College of Harvard University becoming the first deaf-blind person to earn a Bachelor of Arts degree.
  • 1908 - Publishes book "The World I Live In” giving readers an insight into how she felt about the world.
  • 1909 - Joins American Socialist party and writes about political issues including fairer distribution of income and an end to the inequality of Capitalist society.
  • 1910 - Publishes book “The Five-sensed World” that talks about the wisdom and understanding from beyond the senses
  • 1912 - Becomes a convinced socialist and joins Industrial Workers of the World (IWW); as well as advocates socialism, opposing the American involvement in World War I.
  • 1913 - Publishes book "Out of the Dark" that includes a series of essays on socialism.
  • 1915 - Founds the Helen Keller International (HKI) organization along with George A. Kessler, which is devoted to research in vision, health, and nutrition.
  • 1918 - Gets involved in raising funds and awareness for blind charities, improve the living conditions of the blind, who at the time are often badly educated and living in asylums.
  • 1920 - Helps form the American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU)
  • 1927 - Publishes a spiritual autobiography “My Religion” which gets extensively revised and re-issued under the title Light in My Darkness in 1994.
  • 1964 - Awarded with "Presidential Medal of Freedom" by President Lyndon B. Johnson.
  • 1965 - Elected to the National Women's Hall of Fame at the New York World's Fair.
  • 1968 - Dies at the age of 87 years, in Easton, Connecticut.

“When one door of happiness closes, another opens; but often we look so long at the closed door that we do not see the one which has been opened for us.”

― Helen Keller
